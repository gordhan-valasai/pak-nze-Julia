## Section E: Identified inconsistencies

### Capacity inconsistencies
- GW-named technology capacity variables (`installed_capacity_gw`, `new_capacity_gw`) coexist with MW battery and MW-labeled existing-capacity field, causing mixed arithmetic.
- Existing-capacity source key is `_gw` but parameter field is `_mw`.

### Energy/activity inconsistencies
- `annual_activity_mwh` appears to carry GW*hours arithmetic (GWh-like numeric scale) in capacity constraints while treated as MWh in objective, fuel mapping, and extraction.
- Demand key `electricity_grid_gwh` is converted with `*1000` in some places; other service-demand keys used directly.

### Emissions inconsistencies
- Emissions variable names use `_mt`/`_mtco2`, but objective and constraints use `*1e-6` conversion patterns consistent with tonnes base.
- Recent manual `*1000` adjustment in emissions constraint indicates unresolved base-unit mismatch rather than a fully consistent system.

### Cost inconsistencies
- CAPEX/FOM coefficients named `per_kw` but multiplied by `*1000*capacity_gw`, blending conversion logic with possibly misnamed capacity unit.
- Carbon and negative-emission cost terms assume emissions variables are tonnes despite Mt naming.

### Time-period inconsistencies
- Slice-level and annual energy are mostly bridged correctly, but annual activity naming/scale ambiguity causes knock-on dimensional risk in fuel and emissions paths.
